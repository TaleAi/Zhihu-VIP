## 12.津巴多：没有感觉即是地狱
Chapter 4: 


In this chapter we will explore how our sensations contribute to forming perceptions. We experience smell, taste, sight, hearing, touches, and these sensations conspire to produce your perception of the world. Perception is the start of human psychology. 


In preparing to write this chapter I realized how terrible it would be if I could not hear or see or smell or taste sensations around me. These fundamental sensations enable me to connect with the many energy sources bombarding me all the time. In my classes at Stanford I would often invite students to experience what it is like to be blind for a day or deaf for a day by blocking the visual or auditory input. Experiences were profound and I encourage you to try this self- experiment.


在本章中，我们将探讨我们的感觉如何帮助形成知觉。 我们体验到嗅觉、味觉、视觉、听觉、触觉，这些感觉最后联手形成你对世界的感知。 感觉是人类心理活动的开始。


在准备撰写本章时，我意识到，如果我听不到、看不到、闻不到或者感受不到周围的话，那将是多么的可怕！ 这些基本的感觉使我能够与那些整天「轰炸」我的能量源联结。 在斯坦福大学的课堂上，我经常会邀请学生去蒙上眼或带上降噪耳机体验盲人或聋哑人的一天。 他们的经验是非常深刻的，我也鼓励你尝试一下。
