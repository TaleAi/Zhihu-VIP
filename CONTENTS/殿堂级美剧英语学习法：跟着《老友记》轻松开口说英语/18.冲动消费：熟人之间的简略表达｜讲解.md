## 18.冲动消费：熟人之间的简略表达｜讲解
**场景 1：Chandler 回到家中，妻子 Monica 向他展示自己花大手笔买的新靴子。**


![img](https://pic3.zhimg.com/v2-4218f9bec2a13541971eebc2276a653d.webp)

对话文本：


**第一段对话：**


**Monica:** Hey! So what do you think?


**Chandler:** New haircut? Necklace? Dress? Boots? Boots!


**Monica:** Yes! Now, they』re a little more than I usually spend on boots…or rent


**Chandler:** Oh my God!


**Monica:** I know.


**Chandler:** I』m gonna miss being able to afford food.


**Monica:** I』m sorry, they just, they just look so good! And the saleswoman was looking at me like, "Oh, these are way too expensive for you."


**Chandler:** She had a point.


**Rachel:** Hi!


**Monica**: Hey.


**Rachel:** Oh my God! Oh Monica! Those boots are amazing!


**Monica:** They』re mine!


**Chandler:** Yeah well, too bad we』re gonna have to return them.


**Rachel:** Return them?! Shh! They』re gonna hear you!


**Monica:** Honey, I』m not returning them. Okay? I mean I-I know they cost a lot, but I』m going to wear them all the time. You』ll see. Besides, I love the compliments. I mean, have you ever had something that was so beautiful that everyone wants it?


**Chandler:** I have you.


**Monica:** Nice try; I』m keeping the boots.


 


**场景 2：Monica 的新皮鞋并不合脚，但不得不忍痛穿着，一场聚会下来已让她疼痛难耐，Chandler 只好背她回家。**


![img](https://pic2.zhimg.com/v2-ef21e7f74e9f8dc11db810ce57393ebb.webp)

对话文本：


**Chandler:** Y』know, that party wasn』t bad.


**Monica:** Yeah! I didn』t know there』d be dancing. That was a fun surprise!


**Chandler:** I don』t see any uh, cabs. Maybe we should just walk?


**Monica:** Oh no, we can』t walk!


**Chandler:** Come on honey, it』s like fifteen blocks to the subway. Let』s go.


**Monica:** Hey! Do you think that we can get to the subway right there if we climb down through that manhole cover?


**Chandler:** What』s going on?


**Monica:** I can』t walk. Okay? Okay? These boots were a huge mistake!


**Chandler**: What?


**Monica:** Okay you were right! All right, I never should』ve bought them! They』re killing me! One toe at a time!


**Chandler:** So I was right. This is what it feels like to be right. It』s oddly unsettling.


**Monica:** How are we gonna get home? Maybe a piggyback ride?


**Chandler**: Hop on.


**Monica:** Okay. Wait, just give me a second. Let me just get my boots off first. Ah…Ooh…Oohh…Ohh…Oh God…Ohh…Oh…Ohh…Ohhhh…


**Chandler:** Honey, I know you』re in pain right now, but I』m a little turned on.


 


**词汇短语点睛：**


a little more than


字面意思是"只比...多一点点"， 我们要学会在口语中使用到这个短语。比如想表达「一年多一点之前」，就可以说 a little more than a year ago。


l rent


rent 除了做动词表示「出租」，也可以做名词用，通常指「租金；房租」，美剧中经常会听到 pay the rent（付房租），pay the bills（付账单），bill 通常指的是水费、电费、燃气费这种账单。


l way


way too 可以用来修饰形容词，表达的意思是「实在，太……」，起到强调的作用。


return


return 有「退回」的意思，「退货」也可以用 return 这个词。


cab


美语中出租车通常叫做 cab，相当于英式英语中的 taxi。


manhole 


指的是「下水井；(路面下水道的)出入孔」，井盖就是 manhole cover。


piggyback


意思是「背驮；背负」，常见的用法是 give sb. a piggyback ride (背某人)。


hop on


就是「跳上」的意思，Chandler 在对话中表达的意思就是「来吧，跳上来吧」。


give me a second. 


second 有「秒」的意思，字面上說「给我一秒」，实际表达的是「等我一下。」英语里常用 a second ，a minute 来指代「一会儿」，比如 Just a minute.（请稍等）。


 


**地道句型活用：**


She had a point.


have a point 表示的含义是「说的有道理」。关于 point 还有几个常见的用法：


get to the point（说重点） ，例如 


We』re running out of time. Let』s get to the point. 


快没时间了，我们说重点吧。


get/take someone's point（了解某人的意思），例如 


I didn't quite get your point.


我不太懂你的意思。


Nice try.


结合对话的情境，表达的含义是「虽然你试过了（试图说服我退掉靴子），但是我才不上你当呢（可惜你没得逞）。」所以「Nice try.」不是称赞别人的一句话, 反而是嘲讽别人企图达到某种目的却失败且白费工夫的意思。在熟人之间使用通常是开玩笑的口气，意思是「想得美」。


I never should』ve bought them!  


对话中 Monica 表达的意思是「我根本就不应该买这双靴子！」，这里要结合语境来学习 never should『ve done（本不该）和 should『ve done（本该）这两种用法，而不是死记语法规则。


They are killing me!


中文和英文中都会把一些痛苦的事情和死亡联系在一起，这里 Monica 的表达中用了 kill（杀死），意思就是「这双靴子害得我脚疼死了！」再比如 dead（死亡的），在口语中 I am dead. 指的是「我累死了。」


 


**熟人之间说话表达更简略**


本课对话中，Monica 因为脚痛不想走回家，Chandler 说「It』s like fifteen blocks to the subway.」表达的意思是「到地铁站也就差不多 15 个街区远」。这句话其实完整的说法是 It』s like fifteen blocks to the subway station. 之所以 Chandler 没有加上「station」，因为两人作为夫妻，彼此已经很熟悉，所以平时说话往往表达会更简略，也不会影响彼此的理解。再比如后面 Monica 有说到「Maybe a piggyback ride?」说全了就是 Maybe you can give me a piggyback ride?
