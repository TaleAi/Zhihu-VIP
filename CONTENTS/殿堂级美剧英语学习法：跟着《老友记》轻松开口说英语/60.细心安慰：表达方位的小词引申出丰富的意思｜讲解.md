## 60.细心安慰：表达方位的小词引申出丰富的意思｜讲解
【本课对话取自第八季第 20 集】


**场景：参加 baby shower 的 Mrs. Green 决定搬过来跟 Rachel 和 Ross 同住，以便帮忙照顾宝宝。**


![img](https://pic1.zhimg.com/v2-cc230919970365782436050275b342d9.webp)

**对话文本：** 


**Mrs. Green:** …and all those dinosaur knick-knacks you have, Ross, I thought they might be more at home in the garage.


**Ross:** Well we…we don』t have a garage.


**Mrs. Green:** Did I say garage? I meant garbage.


**Ross:** Y』know what? Mrs. Green, maybe it』s not absolutely vital that you live with us.


**Mrs. Green:** Well, Rachel needs help with the baby.


**Rachel:** I do. I really do. I don』t know anything.


**Ross:** I』m-I』m sure that』s not true.


**Rachel:** Oh no? Pheebs? Monica? Do I know anything about babies?


**Phoebe:** No, not a thing.


**Monica:** It』s frightening.


**Ross:** Well uh, y』know what? Even if she doesn』t know anything, I do! I have a son. And his mother and I didn』t live together, and whenever he was with me I took care of him all the time, by myself.


**Mrs. Green:** That』s true. You do have another child.


**Ross:** Yeah.


**Mrs. Green:** With another woman. Have you no control Ross?


**Ross:** That』s a different issue. Uh, the point is, when the baby comes I will be there to…to feed her and bathe her and change her. And more than that I want to do all those things.


**Mrs. Green:** Well then you really don』t need me to live with you.


**Ross:** Yes! Yes, you』re gonna be so missed.


**Mrs. Green:** You』re gonna be a great father.


**Ross:** Well you』re gonna be a wonderful grandma.


**Rachel:** Hello?! I still don』t know what the hell I』m doing!


**Ross:** Oh, come on, every first-time mother feels that way. You』ll—you』re gonna pick it up. Hey! You will! Uh look, y』know when you first came to the city? You were this spoiled helpless little girl who-who still used daddy』s credit cards, remember?


**Rachel:** I hope you』re going somewhere with this.


**Ross:** Look at you! What—You』re-you』re this big executive! You are much more capable than you give yourself credit for. I-I have no doubt you』re gonna be an incredible mother.


**Rachel:** Really?


**Ross:** I』m telling you.


**Rachel:** Thank you. 


**词汇短语点睛：**


dinosaur knick-knacks 


在老友记中 Ross 的职业是一名古生物学教授。这里说的就是 Ross 的那些恐龙小玩意儿， knick-knack 通常指房子里的小装饰品、小摆设。


I thought they might be more at home in the garage.


这句 Mrs. Green 想表达就是这些小玩意儿还是放在车库比较合适。口语中经常会说 make yourself at home，意思就是「不要客气，别太拘谨；像当在自己家一样」at home 引申的意思就是「自在；舒服」。当然 Mrs. Green 后面说到她其实觉得这些东西和垃圾（garbage）放在一起才合适。


It』s not absolutely vital that you live with us.


vital 表示「至关重要的」，这里的意思就相当于 necessary（必要的），这句话说就是「你不一定要和我们住在一起。」


Rachel needs help with the baby.


意思是「Rachel 需要有人帮忙照顾孩子。」help with 就是指「帮助（某人）做…」。


No, not a thing.


表示在照顾孩子方面，Rachel「一件事儿都不懂」。


Have you no control Ross?


这句是比较口语的说法，相当于 Don't you have control Ross?


to feed her and bathe her and change her


指的就是帮忙「为她吃饭、帮她洗澡和换尿布」。


Oh, come on, every first-time mother feels that way. 


first-time mother 指的就是「初为人母；新手妈妈」。这句话的意思就是「每一个初为人母的人都有那样的感觉。」


You』re gonna pick it up.


pick up 除了表示「拿起;拾起」，也有「学会」的意思，这里 Ross 表达的意思就是这些照顾小孩的事情「你可以慢慢学会，不用太担心。」


You are much more capable than you give yourself credit for.


credit 有「功劳」的含义，往往指的是别人对自己的「打分」，这里说的就是 Rachel 给自己打分，整句意思就是「你比你自己认为的更加有能力。」


**地道句型活用：**


You』re gonna be so missed.


表示的就是「你会被想念的。」即「我们都会很想你的。」这里用被动，也是英语常见的用法，不用去特指谁会想念。


I'm telling you.


结合 Ross 肯定的语气，这句话主要是用来强调所说的是真的，Ross 的意思就是「你一定要相信我说的 (我说的没错)。」


**表达方位的小词引申出丰富的意思**


本课对话中，Ross 说到「when the baby comes I will be there to…」，这里的 there 在脑海中对应的其实就是将来会发生的一个场景，Ross 这里的意思就是在将来那个场景（孩子出生后）下，我会帮你...，如果是描述现在的场景，一般是说 I'm here to...,比如假设 Rachel 的孩子已经出生，Ross 想表达现在就来帮她照顾孩子，就可以说 I'm here to help you with the baby. 


要注意的是，在口语中，here 和 there 往往不用来说「这里」和「那里」，而是常常来表示「当下某个时刻/眼前的场景」和「未来某个时刻/脑海中未来的某个场景」。
